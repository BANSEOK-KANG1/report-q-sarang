## Abstract
<title>Abstract</title>  <p>Triple-negative breast cancer (TNBC) remains a serious global health burden for women, with an increasing incidence and a longstanding lack of effective therapeutic strategies. These unmet clinical needs highlight the urgent need to develop novel chemosensitizers and elucidate their underlying molecular mechanisms. Cordycepin(a natural bioactive compound) has been previously reported to exert synergistic anti-tumor effects in various malignant tumors. The present study aims to clarify the chemosensitizing mechanism of cordycepin in TNBC, which is characterized by high invasiveness and strong adaptability to hypoxia. Our results demonstrated that cordycepin significantly enhanced the cytotoxicity of paclitaxel in TNBC cells by inhibiting the HIF-1α/RAD51 positive feedback loop, a key driver of TNBC progression, thereby blocking DNA repair. These findings uncover a novel mechanistic role of cordycepin in enhancing chemosensitivity, providing a promising therapeutic strategy for improving clinical outcomes in patients with TNBC.</p>
