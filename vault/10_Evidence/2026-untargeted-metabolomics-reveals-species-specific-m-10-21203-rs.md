## Abstract
<title>Abstract</title>  <p>  Edible fungi are increasingly valued for their nutritional and medicinal properties, yet systematic comparisons of metabolic profiles among commercially important species remain limited. This study performed untargeted metabolomics using LC-MS/MS to investigate metabolic differences in  <italic>Pholiota nameko</italic>  ,  <italic>Cordyceps militaris</italic>  and  <italic>Lentinula edodes</italic>  . A total of 15 samples (five biological replicates per species) were analyzed. Multivariate (PCA, PLS-DA) and univariate analyses (VIP > 1, P < 0.05) were applied to screen differential metabolites, followed by KEGG pathway enrichment. In total, 2004 metabolites were identified across all samples. Pairwise comparisons revealed 199, 186, and 172 differential metabolites in positive ion mode for the CM vs LE, CM vs PN, and PN vs LE comparisons, respectively. Using a stringent screening strategy based on consistent Log₂FC direction, six characteristic metabolites were identified: C. militaris by high N,N-dimethylguanosine and low hypoxanthine; P. nameko by high 3-(2-hydroxyethyl)indole (tryptophol) and stachydrine; and L. edodes by high ergothioneine and low betaine. KEGG enrichment analysis revealed these differential metabolites are primarily involved in purine metabolism, tryptophan metabolism, histidine metabolism, and glycine, serine and threonine metabolism. This study provides comprehensive metabolic profiling. These uncovered species-specific signatures, which lay a foundation for quality control and functional food development.  </p>
