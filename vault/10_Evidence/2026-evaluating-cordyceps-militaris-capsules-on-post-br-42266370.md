## Abstract
<h4>Background</h4>Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease (COPD) is characterized by a progressive deterioration of lung function, which occurs at its fastest rate in the early stages (GOLD I-II). Preliminary clinical investigation has suggested that <i>Cordyceps militaris</i> capsules can improve post-bronchodilator forced expiratory volume in 1 s (FEV<sub>1</sub>) in mild-to-moderate COPD. However, previous studies were limited by the absence of placebo control. Therefore, this trial is designed to evaluate the efficacy and safety of <i>Cordyceps militaris</i> capsules versus placebo in improving post-bronchodilator FEV<sub>1</sub> in patients with mild-to-moderate stable COPD.<h4>Methods</h4>This study is a multi-center, randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled clinical trial involving 160 eligible patients aged between 40 and 85 years. After a 1-week run-in period, participants are randomized in a 1:1 ratio to receive either <i>Cordyceps militaris</i> capsules or placebo capsules for 24 weeks, and are followed for an additional 24 weeks. The primary outcome measure is the change in post-bronchodilator FEV<sub>1</sub>. Secondary outcome measures comprise additional lung function parameters, such as pre-bronchodilator FEV<sub>1</sub>, pre- and post-bronchodilator measures of forced vital capacity (FVC), FEV<sub>1</sub>/FVC ratio, mid-expiratory flow (MMEF), forced expiratory flow 75% (FEF75), forced expiratory flow 50% (FEF50). In addition, frequency of acute exacerbations, time to the first acute exacerbation, COPD Assessment Test (CAT), Cough and Sputum Assessment Questionnaire (CASA-Q), EuroQol five-dimension five-level questionnaire (EQ-5D-5L), and Modified Medical Research Council dyspnea scale (mMRC) are also included.<h4>Discussion</h4>This study is designed to evaluate the therapeutic potential of <i>Cordyceps militaris</i> capsules and to provide a traditional Chinese medicine intervention for preserving lung function in mild-to-moderate stable COPD.<h4>Clinical trial registration</h4>https://www.chictr.org.cn/, ChiCTR2400086743, identifier, ChiCTR2400086743.
