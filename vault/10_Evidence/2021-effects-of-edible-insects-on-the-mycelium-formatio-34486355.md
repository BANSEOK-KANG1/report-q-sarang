## Abstract
&lt;b&gt;Background and Objective:&lt;/b&gt; &lt;i&gt;Cordyceps militaris &lt;/i&gt;is a potential edible medicinal mushroom which containing various biological activity such as anti-inflammatory, anti-ageing, anti-protozoal and anti-microbial. The compositions of &lt;i&gt;C. militaris&lt;/i&gt; media were composed of carbon source, nitrogen source and other additives. This research aimed to evaluate the effect of edible insects on the &lt;i&gt;C. militaris &lt;/i&gt;mycelium formation. &lt;b&gt;Materials and Methods:&lt;/b&gt; Seven edible insects including &lt;i&gt;Bombyx mori &lt;/i&gt;L., &lt;i&gt;Samia ricini&lt;/i&gt; D., &lt;i&gt;Acheta domesticus&lt;/i&gt; L., &lt;i&gt;Gryllus bimaculatus&lt;/i&gt; De Geer, &lt;i&gt;Tenebrio molitor&lt;/i&gt; L., &lt;i&gt;Rhynchophorus ferrugineus&lt;/i&gt; and &lt;i&gt;Lethocerus indicus&lt;/i&gt; were used as nitrogen source supplemented in Potato Dextrose Agar (PDA) and the mycelium formation of each edible insects at day 7, 14 and 21 were recorded. &lt;b&gt;Results:&lt;/b&gt; The results of nitrogen source from a boiled edible insect at day 21 indicated that the highest colony diameter at 88.00 mm was obtained when cultured with PDA+&lt;i&gt;B. mori&lt;/i&gt; L. The results of nitrogen source from a dried edible insect at day 21 presented that the highest diameter at 84.33 mm was obtained from cultured using PDA+&lt;i&gt;A. domesticus&lt;/i&gt; L. &lt;b&gt;Conclusion:&lt;/b&gt; The suitable boiled and dried edible insects for the supplement in PDA were boiled &lt;i&gt;B. mori&lt;/i&gt; L. and dried &lt;i&gt;A. domesticus &lt;/i&gt;L. This is the first report about PDA supplemented with edible insects that can be increased the &lt;i&gt;C. militaris&lt;/i&gt; mycelium formation which the initial stage that important for &lt;i&gt;C. militaris &lt;/i&gt;cultivation.
